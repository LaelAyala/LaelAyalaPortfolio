IUL Internship Summer 2024

I researched how changing the ball burnishing pressure, step over, and tool path affects the properties of metal sheets produced by Laser Powder Bed Fusion.For more details, click View Project

During the summer of 2024, I was accepted into the Ruhr Fellowship program which selects 11 students from the U.S and matches them with internships in Germany. I was a research intern at the Institute of Lightweight Technology and Forming Components (IUL) at TU Dortmund.

During my internship with the IUL, I worked under Dr. Hamed Dardaei to analyze the effects of ball-burnishing on the mechanical properties of additively manufactured flat sheets. Using the HG19 tool, I varied pressure, step-over, and burnishing strategy and measured the changes in surface roughness, waviness, and residual stresses of the steel samples. I also evaluated surface hardness with the Vickers test and measured tensile strength. I learned about uniform elongation percentage, and its relation to ultimate tensile strength.

Beyond technical skills, I developed soft skills by creating effective presentations and collaborating with diverse colleagues. This experience was invaluable, and I will carry these lessons into my academic and professional career.
